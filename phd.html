My PhD Research

Exploring Potential Energy Surfaces with Monte Carlo Threshold Mapping

PhD Candidate

Project Overview

My doctoral research in the Day Group at the University of Southampton focuses on investigating the crystal potential energy surface (PES) of organic molecules. By employing advanced Monte Carlo threshold (MCT) mapping, my project aims to bridge the gap in crystal structure prediction (CSP).

While traditional CSP is highly effective at identifying the local minima of a crystal landscape, it does not naturally reveal the energy barriers between these forms. By obtaining these barrier heights, we can rationalise the appearance of experimental polymorphs and (hopefully) predict the appearance of new polymorphs that would otherwise be difficult to compute with traditional CSP workflows.

Interactive Threshold Simulator

Explore the mechanics of the Monte Carlo Threshold (MCT) algorithm in real-time. In an MCT simulation, a random walker explores a potential energy landscape by taking random steps. However, any step that exceeds a predefined Energy Lid (the threshold) is strictly rejected. As you raise the Energy Lid, the walker gains access to higher barriers, allowing it to cross transition states and connect previously isolated crystal structures (polymorphs). This simulation starts the MCT trajectory from the Form I local minimum of a hypothetical crystal landscape. Your task is to adjust the Energy Lid and observe how the walker navigates the landscape, discovering new polymorphs and connecting them through energy barriers.

Key PhD Milestones & Projects

Phase 1
PAH Crystal Landscapes

Mapped the potential energy surfaces of three polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons: phenanthrene, pyrene, and perylene. We tested three distinct potential models, proving that energy barriers between experimental polymorphs remain highly consistent regardless of the force field model used.

Phase 2
Large-Scale Rigid Mapping

Conducted a massive Monte Carlo threshold study spanning 55 small rigid molecules. Applying unsupervised K-means clustering to the disconnectivity graph descriptors, we successfully categorized the crystal landscapes into the distinct structural classes originally theorized by David Wales.

Phase 3
Intramolecular Flexibility & ROY

Expanded the Monte Carlo threshold algorithm to include intramolecular conformational moves, allowing us to explore flexible crystal landscapes. Applied this to the iconic ROY molecule.
